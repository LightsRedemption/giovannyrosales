Hi, my name is Giovanny Orlando Rosales or Gio for short. I am currentyly a first PhD student at New Mexico State University. I work in two different regimes of Astrophysics: Galactic Archeology and Astereoseismolgy, so galaxies and stars! I am interested in the formation history of the Milky Way
and other galaxies in the universe. I take advtange of a galaxy's chemical abundances, orbital dynamics, and stellar population to understand how a galaxy formed throughout its life. I am also interested in stellar oscillations and compact objects, more specifically how stellar oscillations can be 
used to constrain stellar evolution that leads to compact object binary mass-gap objects.

I did my undergraduate studies at the University of California, Irivine, where I studied under Dr. Manoj Kaplinghat, researching how dark matter halos affect the rotation of dwarf irregular galaxies. Irinve, California. The physics department at UCI showed me how important it is to do outreach for my community and show people there place in the universe. Irvine is also where I noticed the struggles of academia and the importance of keeping retention rates high in physics and astronomy. I am an 
advocate for minorities in STEM and Irvine was the starting ground for being more inclusive and encouraging minorities to pursue a degree in STEM.

After my undergraduate, I decided to get my masters degree in Computational Physics at California State University, Long Beach, while focusing on Astrophysics research under Dr. Joel Zinn. At Long Beach, I studied Astereoseismology which is the study of stellar oscilltions. It is a useful method 
for determining stellar propertities like mass, age, and chemical composition. More specifically, I studied solar-like oscillators in mass-gap binary systems to understand wether or not a companion compact object was a neutron star or a blach hole. It was during my graduate work at Long Beach where
I truly fell in love with astrophysics research. Long Beach was also the place I became more interdisciplinary, making friends in different fields and collobarting on different projects with the engineers there.
